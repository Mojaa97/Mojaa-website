Most people know one date. July 31. They do not know the other five. Here is every ITR deadline for AY 2026-27 and what happens if you miss each one.

**What is AY 2026-27.** Financial Year 2025-26 is April 1 2025 to March 31 2026. That is when you earned the income. Assessment Year 2026-27 is when you file the return. If you are filing in 2026 for income earned in FY 2025-26 you are filing for AY 2026-27.

---

**All Six Deadlines**

July 31 2026. Individuals, salaried employees, pensioners, freelancers, HUFs not requiring audit. This is the main deadline. Miss it and you lose the ability to carry forward most losses.

October 31 2026. Businesses and professionals whose accounts are required to be audited under the Income Tax Act. Companies, firms with turnover above 1 crore, professionals with receipts above 50 lakh.

November 30 2026. Taxpayers with international transactions required to submit a Transfer Pricing Report in Form 3CEB.

December 31 2026. Belated Return under Section 139(4). If you missed July 31 you can still file here with a late fee. Also the deadline for Revised Returns under Section 139(5).

March 31 2028. ITR-U Updated Return filed within 1 year of end of AY. Additional tax of 25% on tax plus interest applies.

March 31 2029. ITR-U Updated Return filed in 2nd year after end of AY. Additional tax rises to 50% on tax plus interest.

---

**The 7 ITR Forms**

ITR-1 Sahaj. Resident individuals only. Salary or pension, one house property, other sources like interest. Total income up to 50 lakh. Agricultural income up to 5000. Cannot be used by NRIs, those with capital gains, directors of companies, or holders of unlisted equity shares.

ITR-2. Individuals and HUFs with capital gains, more than one house property, foreign income or foreign assets, income above 50 lakh, or NRI status. No business or profession income.

ITR-3. Individuals and HUFs with income from business or profession. Partners in a firm for personal income. Those with presumptive income who do not opt for the presumptive scheme.

ITR-4 Sugam. Individuals, HUFs, and firms other than LLP opting for presumptive taxation. Section 44AD for business with turnover up to 3 crore at 8 or 6 percent deemed profit. Section 44ADA for professionals with receipts up to 75 lakh at 50 percent deemed profit. Section 44AE for transport operators. Total income up to 50 lakh. Resident individuals only.

ITR-5. Firms including LLP, Association of Persons, Bodies of Individuals, Business Trusts, Investment Funds.

ITR-6. Companies other than those claiming exemption under Section 11 as charitable or religious trusts.

ITR-7. Charitable and religious trusts under Section 139(4A), political parties under 139(4B), scientific research institutions and news agencies under 139(4C), universities and colleges under 139(4D), business trusts under 139(4E), investment funds under 139(4F).

**What Happens When You Miss July 31**

Late filing fee under Section 234F. Five thousand rupees if total income exceeds 5 lakh. One thousand rupees if total income is 5 lakh or below.

Interest under Section 234A. One percent per month on unpaid tax from the due date to the actual filing date.

Loss of carry-forward. You cannot carry forward capital losses, business losses, or speculative losses. You can still carry forward house property losses even in a belated return.

No refund interest. You lose interest on your refund for the delayed period.

---

**Belated Return: December 31 2026**

Filed under Section 139(4). This is a complete return filed after the original due date. The late fee under Section 234F still applies. You cannot carry forward most losses. But a belated return is fully valid and accepted.

---

**Revised Return: December 31 2026**

Filed under Section 139(5). Use this if you already filed on time or as a belated return but made an error. You can revise multiple times before the deadline. No additional late fee for revising. Common reasons: wrong income figure, missed deduction under 80C or 80D, incorrect TDS credit, wrong ITR form filed, bank account details wrong.

You cannot revise a return that has already been assessed under Section 143(3) or one filed as defective and not corrected.

---

**ITR-U Updated Return: Up to March 31 2029**

Introduced in Budget 2022 under Section 139(8A). This gives taxpayers a longer window to correct or file returns they missed entirely.

When you can file ITR-U. You did not file the original return. You filed but missed reporting some income. Income was reported under the wrong head. Wrong deductions or exemptions were claimed. Assets or foreign income were omitted.

When you cannot file ITR-U. The updated return results in a refund or an increase in refund. The updated return reduces your tax liability. A search, survey, or assessment has been initiated by the department. Prosecution proceedings have been started.

Additional tax on ITR-U. Filed by March 31 2028 which is within 1 year of end of AY 2026-27: additional tax is 25 percent of tax payable plus interest. Filed between April 1 2028 and March 31 2029: additional tax rises to 50 percent of tax payable plus interest.

---

**New vs Old Tax Regime for AY 2026-27**

New regime is the default from FY 2023-24. Simplified slabs, lower rates, standard deduction of 75000 for salaried individuals, no deductions for 80C 80D HRA LTA and most others.

Old regime has higher rates but allows all deductions and exemptions. You must opt in explicitly while filing.

Salaried individuals can switch between regimes every year at the time of filing. Business owners who opt out of the new regime can switch back only once in their lifetime.

---

**E-Verification: 30 Days**

Filing is not complete until you e-verify. You have 30 days from the date of filing to e-verify. Options: Aadhaar OTP, net banking, bank account EVC, demat account EVC, or Digital Signature Certificate. If not verified within 30 days the return is treated as not filed.

---

**3 Things Most Taxpayers Get Wrong**

First: filing late because they assume the department will extend the deadline. Extensions are not guaranteed. File before July 31.

Second: not cross-checking Form 26AS and AIS before filing. Mismatches trigger notices.

Third: thinking ITR-U is a free second chance. It is not. You pay 25 to 50 percent extra on what you owe. File correctly the first time.
